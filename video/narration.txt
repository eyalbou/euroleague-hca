Do home teams in EuroLeague really win more? And if they do, why?

I analyzed ten seasons of EuroLeague basketball. Twenty eight hundred games. One point one million play by play events. All of it running locally in Cursor, on my laptop.

Here's the headline. EuroLeague home teams win one point seven five times more often than road teams. They score three point eight eight more points per game on average. That's real. It's big. It's stable across ten seasons.

So the question becomes: why?

The old answer, from Moskowitz and Wertheim's book Scorecasting, is referee bias. They showed it for the NBA. It was the single biggest driver of home advantage.

I tested it for Europe. Across sixty one referees with at least thirty games each, I compared home versus away foul calls for every single one. After Holm correction for sixty one simultaneous tests, the number of statistically biased referees came out to zero. The raw outlier count matched pure chance almost exactly. European basketball officiating, within our statistical power, is neutral.

Okay. So what is driving the home edge?

I pulled every play by play event and computed points per possession for every offensive action. Home teams score zero point zero four nine more points per possession than road teams. That tiny per-possession edge, multiplied by about eighty possessions a game, explains ninety four percent of the observed home advantage.

The story isn't one big thing. It's a thousand small things. Home teams are slightly better at every single source action. Not one. Every single one. Nineteen out of nineteen trackable possession starters. That's the attribution.

For the second order chains -- what I call Storylines -- I found that after a home team gets a defensive rebound, the next three events favor the home team in a way that cascades. Momentum isn't a myth. It's just micro.

And then COVID. During the twenty-twenty to twenty twenty one season, games played in empty arenas. A natural experiment you could not run deliberately. Home advantage dropped by almost two points per game. When the fans came back, so did the edge. So crowd presence matters. But it's layered on top of everything else, not the whole thing.
